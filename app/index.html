Mantrapp is a personal productivity method based on your favourite Apps. It's actually an updated implementation of GTD (Getting Things Done).

The idea is about moving planned tasks and projects out of the mind by recording them externally. This allows one to focus attention on taking action on tasks, instead of on recalling them.

Mantrapp is focused on helping you to get a more balanced life. It takes advantage of the APPs era and the ubiquous access to our personal information through our mobiles, tablets, laptops...



  • Choose the processes you want to balance
  • Follow 3 daily workflows
  • Configure your apps accordingly

    Processes

    Processes should be like a mantra in your day by day. They are defined by a single letter (a,b,c,d,e...). Every process represents a related group of tasks you do very often. If we are able to improve a bit of them it will have a huge impact on our lives.

    Processes will serve us as the main sections in every of the workflows and in every of our apps.

    For example we will have the section b "body" in Wunderlist where we will have stored the tasks we have to do for taking care of our body, in Evernote we will have the section b for example for a log of the medicines we are using and also in our laptop or phone we could have some apps related to health or sport, also in the section b.

    Workflow

    In GTD there are 2 defined workflow, the workflow for Actionable stuff (things that can be converted to tasks), and the workflow for Non-Actionable stuff. For the first workflow I use Wunderlist while for the second it is Evernote.

    Mantrapp adds an extra workflow called processable that includes all the stuff that can be processed by it's own app. For example the list of movies you wanna watch could be an actionable task and go to Wunderlist but it's much more convenient to store them into the IMDB app wich is meant for that.
